Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the ability to use digital technologies responsibly, safely, and ethically. It's about how people behave online and how they understand their rights and responsibilities in digital spaces.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the rules, regulations, and legal principles that govern how people use technology, the internet, and digital information.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy is a set of rules that explains how computers, networks, and internet services may be used responsibly and appropriately within an organization (such as a school, workplace, or company).

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or words without giving proper credit, and presenting them as your own.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair academic advantage or misrepresents their own work.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

Rules for AI-Generated Content are guidelines that explain how AI tools (like chatbots or image generators) may be used ethically, legally, and responsibly, especially in education and professional settings.
