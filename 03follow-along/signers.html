Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776) are well known because of their later roles in U.S. history—or because they were already prominent figures at the time. Here are the standouts:

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress and famous for his large, bold signature. His name became synonymous with the word “signature.”
Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence who later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned scientist, inventor, and diplomat who helped secure vital French support during the American Revolution.
Samuel Adams
Influential revolutionary organizer and political thinker, closely associated with events like the Boston Tea Party.
Roger Sherman
The only Founding Father to sign all four major founding documents, including the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution.
Robert Morris
A wealthy merchant who helped finance the Revolutionary War and is often called the “Financier of the Revolution.”
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The wealthiest signer of the Declaration of Independence and the last surviving signer, dying in 1832.

Altogether, 56 men signed the Declaration, ranging from lawyers and merchants to farmers and politicians—but the ones above tend to get the most historical spotlight.
